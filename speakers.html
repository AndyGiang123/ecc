Speakers

Keynote Speaker

Yin J. Li is a licensed marriage and family therapist. She grew up with working class immigrant parents, so she was well versed in the stigma behind needing support. She went to her first therapy appointment in her early 20s, not knowing it would change the whole course of her life. Li ended up leaving her 15 year career in investment banking and technology. In hopes that Asians and Asian Americans would start to seek therapy as a means to heal, and recognizing that the Asian community often suffers in silence, she started Asians Do Therapy.

Workshop Series: Personal

Tiffany Yu is the CEO and founder of Diversability and Awesome Foundation Disability Chapter. Through her passion in advocating for mental health and disability she has been featured in Forbes, The Wall Street Journal and spoken at TEDx and Harvard to further her vision. Her mission is to increase intersectional disability representation and democratize visibility. She values action through learning, honesty, justice, creativity, and leadership.

Michelle Yang is a writer and a speaker who has worked in the creative industry for professional copywriters and in nonprofit sectors. She is a passionate blogger who writes about mental health, feminism, and Asian American identity. Although she had previously struggled with bipolar disorder, her unique experiences capture what it means to endure intergenerational trauma from immigrant parents and overcome the stigma of mental illness in Asian Americans. Her lifestyle as a parent with a mental illness also provides a different perspective about her personal worries that overlap with how to teach the next Asian generation, including her own child. She stresses the importance of separating trauma from culture to embrace your self-worth and identity.

Stephanie J. Wong is a licensed Clinical Psychologist who strives to de-stigmatize mental illness. She assists individuals in taking the first steps to seeking help. In Stephanie's free time, she hosts The Color of Success Podcast, which centers around sharing the stories of Asian business owners' and their journeys' of rising to success. She discusses the challenges they have faced, specifically their effects on mental health and their ability to cope and maintain mental resilience.

Workshop Series: Interpersonal

A former member of the Intercollegiate Taiwanese American Student Association and Harvard alumni, Ben Wei is an entrepreneur, educator, and advocate for racial injustice. He began his nonprofit organizations-Asians Fighting Injustice and The COVID Foundation- in light of the Atlanta Spa shootings and continuous Asian hate crimes. He actively participates in rallies and media discussions to dispel the hatred that progresses within the Asian community. Passionate about racial equality, social impact, and tech, Wei hopes to enlighten college students and young adults about how to use their social media platforms to speak up against anti-Asian violence and actively disseminate hate crimes.

Having faced constant discrimination not only in regards to her race but also her age throughout her career as a reporter, today, May Lee is a strong advocate against anti-asian bias and is an active voice for the Asian American Community. Following her success as the first female anchor of Korean descent for CNN, May Lee utilizes her instagram, podcast, youtube and talk show “The May Lee Show” to explore and voice her opinions in regards to AAPI issues. Modeled based off “The Oprah Winfrey Show”, “The May Lee Show” explores and discusses many critical issues surrounding politics, social problems: divorce, child trafficking, infidelity and soft issues including fashion and celebrities.

Jason Y. Lee is the CEO and founder of Jubilee Media, a digital media startup, who left his consulting job at Bain & Company to pursue his dreams of creating content on YouTube. Lee took on a creative outlet through his video company to inspire passionate and driven millennials. Jubilee has garnered over 1 billion and 6+ million subscribers to push people to think and challenge others through empathy and living for the human good. He hopes to change the narrative of how Asian Americans understand one another through experiences, opinions, and perspectives. The movement of empathy strengthens Lee's goal to bridge the gap between the younger and older generation's upbringings.

Workshop Series: Career

Gloria Chao is a Taiwanese American YA author, known for Rent a Boyfriend, Our Wayward Fate, and American Panda. In her novels, she writes about the struggles of expressing individuality as an Asian American. The inspiration she got for Our Wayward Fate was drawn from the miscommunication she experienced with her own parents, as they expected her to fulfill the dentistry career path. However, Chao was able to truly communicate with parents and find her identity once she switched to full time writing.

Victoria Tsai is the founder and Chief Treasure Hunter of Tatcha, a Japanese inspired skincare company based in San Francisco. This luxury brand combines self-care customs and rituals combined with advanced formulas. Tatcha partnered up Room to Read, a non-profit focused on improving girls’ education and children’s literacy in Asia and Africa.
