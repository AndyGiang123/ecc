Speakers

Keynote Speaker

Yin J. Li is a licensed marriage and family therapist. She grew up with working class immigrant parents, so she was well versed in the stigma behind needing support. She went to her first therapy appointment in her early 20s, not knowing it would change the whole course of her life. Li ended up leaving her 15 year career in investment banking and technology. In hopes that Asians and Asian Americans would start to seek therapy as a means to heal, and recognizing that the Asian community often suffers in silence, she started Asians Do Therapy.

Workshop Series: Personal

Stephanie J. Wong is a licensed Clinical Psychologist who strives to de-stigmatize mental illness. She assists individuals in taking the first steps to seeking help. In Stephanie's free time, she hosts The Color of Success Podcast, which centers around sharing the stories of Asian business owners' and their journeys' of rising to success. She discusses the challenges they have faced, specifically their effects on mental health and their ability to cope and maintain mental resilience.

Michelle Yang is a writer and a speaker who has worked in the creative industry for professional copywriters and in nonprofit sectors. She is a passionate blogger who writes about mental health, feminism, and Asian American identity. Although she had previously struggled with bipolar disorder, her unique experiences capture what it means to endure intergenerational trauma from immigrant parents and overcome the stigma of mental illness in Asian Americans. Her lifestyle as a parent with a mental illness also provides a different perspective about her personal worries that overlap with how to teach the next Asian generation, including her own child. She stresses the importance of separating trauma from culture to embrace your self-worth and identity.

Workshop Series: Interpersonal

A former member of the Intercollegiate Taiwanese American Student Association and Harvard alumni, Ben Wei is an entrepreneur, educator, and advocate for racial injustice. He began his nonprofit organizations-Asians Fighting Injustice and The COVID Foundation- in light of the Atlanta Spa shootings and continuous Asian hate crimes. He actively participates in rallies and media discussions to dispel the hatred that progresses within the Asian community. Passionate about racial equality, social impact, and tech, Wei hopes to enlighten college students and young adults about how to use their social media platforms to speak up against anti-Asian violence and actively disseminate hate crimes.

A former Harvard alumna, Emma Teng is an M.I.T professor of Asian Civilization and a published author of infamous books such as “Taiwan’s Imagined Geography: Chinese Colonial Travel Writing and Pictures, 1683-1985 (2004)” and “Eurasian: Mixed Identities in the United States, China and Hong Kong, 1842-1943 (2013)”. Her work and past research focuses on interracial mixing and the generational gap of mixed families. She currently serves on the International Advisory Board for the International Chinese Language Program at National Taiwan University, and the Faculty Advisory Board for the North American Taiwan Studies Association. Her robust knowledge on Asian American history and contemporary issues are vital to understand how it shapes the modern Asian American identity. She will address the model minority syndrome and how the transnational migration of immigrants has created a “Global Chinese” identity, and what effects this has in modern society.

As both a Rutgers TASA alumna and Taiwanese American, Grace Chi is a Co-founder of Pulsedive Threat Intelligence, a cybersecurity company that helps organizations combat security posture. She is an experienced guest speaker who has mentored numerous college students in navigating job placement, such as intersectionality and embracing interdisciplinary perspectives in a career. Chi has also presented cyber-specific presentations to encourage underrepresented groups to consider a security career path. She hopes to provide insight into her career switch from marketing to starting a cyber security company, the obstacles that are faced for students to break into this niche field, and how to combat the reputations that are negatively enforced within STEM fields.
